"Perhaps, at the extreme--but everything is always decided at the extreme--we will one day have to face the fact (decouvrir) that the god is essentially distinguishable by nothing save the extreme strangeness of his coming. Euripides:

Numerous are the forms of the divine,
and numerous, the unexpected decision of the gods,
What was expected does not come about,
but for the unexpected, the god has found the means.

(Jean-Luc Nancy, The Inoperative Community, translated by Peter Connor, Lisa Garbus, Michael Holland, and Simona Sawhney (Minneapolis: University of Minnesota Press, 1991), P. 125)
